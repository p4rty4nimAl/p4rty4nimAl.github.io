Ignorance I laid still, in a field of grass, The curve of a lifetime forgotten, Traceable, The grass of a thousand lives before me, Each identical under the gaze of the masses, Each with such story, Such individuality, A tree stands, squats: the horizon calls, You ignore it, As you distance, trees disappear, Forests give way to cities; Give way to the trap, Hundreds of trees stand, still, Surrounded by smoke and steel, A breath of fresh air no more than a myth, I lay once again in this former field, You see me, silently approach; I think: I know your words; But you just walk away, Once more.
